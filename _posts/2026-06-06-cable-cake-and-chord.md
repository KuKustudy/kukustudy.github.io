### Cables 🔌

Recently at work, I got the chance to handle some cables, in particular network cables and fibre optic cables.

I got the chance to play around with a network cable tester, which was fun! The tester comes in two parts. I have attached an image below to illustrate what it looks like. Both parts have an interface for the network cable. Basically, it tests the connectivity of a cable to see if it works properly. You attach one end of the cable to one part of the tester, then attach the other end of the cable to the second part. If the cable works properly, the lights should flash for all 6 indicators, indicating that all 6 twists in the cable are working properly.

![network cable testor](/assets/images/network_cable_testor.jpg)

While it may seem unnecessary to test it when you can simply connect devices at both ends of the cable to check connectivity, the tester provides a simple and portable way to test cables on a site that you may not be familiar with.

Alongside network cables, I also taught myself how to test the connectivity of a fibre optic cable. By “training,” I mean watching a great YouTube video. Testing a fibre optic cable is more straightforward. The tester only has one part, which acts like a torch that emits light through the cable to the other end. If you can see the light at the other end, it means the cable is working fine.

Another important reason why we need cable testers is that, in office or warehouse settings, it is common not to see the whole connection in one place. Instead, you may see one interface on the server rack and the other interface somewhere in the office. Usually, they are labelled so we know they are matched, but it is still important to test them to help us understand the connection setup onsite.

I love doing hands-on things, so this was great fun for me!

### Chord 🎸

Today I have bought myself a guitar!!!! I have always loved music and would love to learn a musical instrument, but I have absolutely no background knowledge nor any training relating to music. I always held myself back when it came to it, but I have been thinking about this for at least two years. It all started with a free guitar lesson that I saw online about two weeks ago, and I attended. That was not a very enjoyable class and was definitely a humbling experience. My fingers hurt, and the people in the class were so far ahead of me.

After that lesson, the next Saturday I had another commitment that prevented me from attending another lesson, so I decided maybe that was the end of my guitar journey. I even threw away the chord sheet that I got from the lesson.

Someday after the free guitar lesson, I had dinner with my best friend — hot pot is so good — and we strolled to Melbourne Central. It happened that there was a busker performing outside the State Library. It also happened that the busker was a great guitar player and singer. It was such a nice, cold, enjoyable, lovely night. And I could not help but reimagine myself with a guitar, so I booked another guitar lesson this week and got myself this! 

I honestly do not know whether this will be a great failure or success, but there is a saying that I saw online and keep reminding myself of:

“Congrats on your failure. Most people do not even try.”
