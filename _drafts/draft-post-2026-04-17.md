This is the second blog of the website.

It has been so long since the last time I posted. Looking back at my first blog, I am quite ambious in making this a vibrant digital garden by Feb hahaha. It is already mid April, and there are so many things have happened in between. I would absolutely want to share them all!

Spoiler alert, I did get some stunning photos taken in my graduation ceremony. The process was exhausting, but honestly I would do that again. It feels great to plan everything and close up the chapter with family and friends. Shout out to my friend Lisa, Freya and Danielle! They are the best friend and photographer ever!

After graduation ceremony, I jump directly into travelling and have had the greatest fun in life. School, job application and the ever-down economy were swept out from my mind.

I went to Tasmania with my friend, saw dolphins, woke up by the sea, went star-gazing and cooking in the middle of nowhere. I went to Hong Kong by myself, a home city in my heart, went to the live music bar in 兰桂坊, explored the authentic dim sim culture, stopping by street buskers. I went on a group trip with my primary school friends, one of them started working, but everything felt the same about them. We were silly, and it is good to be silly. I went to Malaysia with my younger cousin, it is the first time I learnt how to take care of others in a trip. I visited my uni friends in Malaysia which were super excited, the food and people were so great! I went to Hokkaido, Japan by myself, first time solo trip to a foreign country. There was a lot of uncertainties, I was taken by the view, a city covered in snow is just so freaking romantic. I went to a live jazz bar, but the best part was the convenience stores, I love Seicomart! Can never visit too many thrift stores, I will probably visit again in summer time. And 100 days of Duolingo did not help my broken Japanese..

Travelling is so fun, so many great memories. This period of time is a pillar in my life, red eye flights and 1 hour walking with huge luggages are all worth it!

I did so many travellings in a short amount of time. Because of that, I spent only 3-4 weeks staying in my home town city this time, but what really struck me after all those travellings is that I love my home town city even more. I start appreciating the quietness, and really it feels that all the great times and places I was looking for in other cities and countries, really, I can find that peace in my home town already. It is a really weird feeling when I realised that.

<img src="/assets/images/jiangmen.jpg" width="300">

Shifting subject, let’s do some update on my career. This year late March, I have started my first ever job as an IT supporter specialist for a local company in Melbourne. Sometimes when times in uni flash back in my mind, having a job still feels unreal to me, becoming an actual adult still feels far. This is my fourth working week, soon I will lose track of the number. I am still grabbing pieces of information here and there, trying to figure out how the corporation works, how does the business run. I am so lucky though, my manager, my colleagues and bosses are super friendly, it is easy to carry a smile around the office, days feel so fast and slow sometimes.

Slowly adjusting into the corporation life, but the first work week though, I felt inadequate, a shortage in knowledge. The last time I felt that was the time when I did my first hackathorn. The feeling of being sat aside and not being able to help is the worst feeling in the world. It sucks but this is the situation when people learn and grow, I am convinced that I will outgrow this.

So life in general is not bad at all. With my new 9 to 5 job, I feel a distinction between life and work which is super different from uni where study, classes and life seem to blend perfectly. In a fast moving life in the past few months, the only one continuous trajectory in life for me is swimming. Even in travel, I tried to find time and places to swim, having a period of time with myself and the water made life wonderful! It's great to have something to count on whenever I feel down, I highly recommended.

And meeting up with friends! I actually met my uni friends today, yes, on a Friday night, what a perfect timing! Catching up on each others' life update, I treasure it more than ever. It was so happy but also low key I feel so sad that we are all heading to new direction in life and meeting up might be hard in the future. The only certain thing is that we are still trying to figure it out, and it is good to know people are struggling just like you hahahah.

To wrap this up, I just wanna say stepping into a new life is not easy for me, it is probably not easy for everyone. When setting boundaries for myself feels tough, when sorting out life feels tough, when the world around you is messy and overblown, and you feel shutted, it is important to remind yourself that we still have the ability to read and write, to verify your own feeling and express it authentically.

What's next? My current goal is to write a blog for every month? I don't know why I put the question mark here but we shall see haha. I plan to study for the CompTIA Network+ certificate, I made a study plan which is going well so far, maybe I will write a post on that once I finished. I been doing some stuff with AI, if I have something excited to share, I might write a post as well. Other than that, I will just keep swimming :)
